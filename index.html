Shaoqi QI | 戚少奇

I am a Ph.D. researcher in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Liverpool. My research interests center on 3D computer vision, with a focus on monocular multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM — enabling multiple cameras to collaboratively reconstruct a single, geometrically and photometrically consistent scene from RGB video alone, without depth sensors.

I am especially interested in compact submap communication between agents, geometry- and appearance-aware loop verification, and occupancy-aware Gaussian fusion under monocular scale ambiguity. My recent work has been released on the project page of MAGS-SLAM📈.

Beyond research, I care about reproducible, modular systems that survive contact with real video. I am always open to collaborations on collaborative mapping, dense SLAM, and Gaussian Splatting in the wild.

Featured Research

My research interests center on 3D computer vision, specifically including multi-agent dense SLAM, 3D Gaussian Splatting, monocular scale recovery, and collaborative photorealistic reconstruction.

MAGS-SLAM: Monocular Multi-Agent Gaussian Splatting SLAM for Geometrically and Photometrically Consistent Reconstruction
Shaoqi QI et al.
Under Review, 2026
Project Page

The first RGB-only multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM for collaborative photorealistic scene reconstruction. We introduce compact submap communication, geometry- and appearance-aware loop verification, and occupancy-aware Gaussian fusion, together with ReplicaMultiAgent Plus — a benchmark for collaborative Gaussian SLAM.
