Qi Shao

I am a Ph.D. student in Control Science and Engineering at Harbin Engineering University, and a jointly trained Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Liverpool. I received my bachelor's degree from the College of Intelligent Systems Science and Engineering, Harbin Engineering University.

My research interests include 3D scene representation, simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM), multi-robot perception, and embodied intelligence. My current work asks how robots can build shared spatial representations that are geometrically accurate, semantically meaningful, and useful for downstream navigation, manipulation, and collaboration.

I am especially interested in hybrid 3D representations that connect classical geometry, neural maps such as 3D Gaussian Splatting, and foundation-model priors, enabling multi-robot systems to reason about dynamic environments and coordinate perception and action through vision-centered 3D understanding.

I also serve as a Huawei Developer Evangelist, sharing technical practices around Ascend AI, intelligent systems, and project-based AI development with student and developer communities.

Featured Project / Ascend AI Developer Stories, 2023

Ascend AI-Based Moderate Rice Processing Solution

I worked with my team on Shengzhi Shimi, an Ascend AI-powered solution for intelligent rice processing. The system combines Ascend computing, MindSpore, and edge-cloud heterogeneous collaboration to inspect rice quality during production and support more stable, moderate processing.

The project targets small, adhesive grain features such as rice embryos and achieves 98.5% segmentation accuracy, while detecting quality indicators including whiteness, embryo retention, chalkiness, and embryo integrity.
