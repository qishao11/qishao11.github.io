About

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Liverpool working on 3D computer vision. My research focuses on monocular multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM — enabling multiple cameras to collaboratively reconstruct a single, geometrically and photometrically consistent scene from RGB video alone, without depth sensors.

I am especially interested in: (i) compact submap communication that lets agents share scene knowledge without exchanging raw observations or dense maps; (ii) geometry- and appearance-aware loop verification for robust inter-agent association; and (iii) occupancy-aware Gaussian fusion that resolves monocular scale ambiguity in collaborative settings. I care about reproducible, modular systems that survive contact with real video.

Outside research: long-distance running, film photography, and (occasionally) cooking experiments that should not be rerun.

MAGS-SLAM: Monocular Multi-Agent Gaussian Splatting SLAM for Geometrically and Photometrically Consistent Reconstruction

Shaoqi Qi et al.

Under review, 2026

The first RGB-only multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM for collaborative photorealistic scene reconstruction. Compact submap communication, geometry- and appearance-aware loop verification, and occupancy-aware Gaussian fusion address monocular scale ambiguity in collaborative settings. We also introduce ReplicaMultiAgent Plus, a benchmark for collaborative Gaussian SLAM.
