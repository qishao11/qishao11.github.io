Qi Shao

I am a Ph.D. student in Control Science and Engineering at Harbin Engineering University, and a jointly trained Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Liverpool. I received my bachelor's degree from the College of Intelligent Systems Science and Engineering, Harbin Engineering University.

My research interests include 3D scene reconstruction, simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM), multi-sensor fusion, and robot perception. My current work focuses on robust localization and dense reconstruction for robotic systems, especially visual-inertial-LiDAR SLAM and 3D Gaussian Splatting.

Recently, I have been working on MAGS-SLAM, a monocular multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM system for collaborative photorealistic scene reconstruction from RGB video.

Representative Academic Work

My research interests center on 3D computer vision, specifically including multi-agent dense SLAM, 3D Gaussian Splatting, monocular scale recovery, and collaborative photorealistic reconstruction.

MAGS-SLAM: Monocular Multi-Agent Gaussian Splatting SLAM for Geometrically and Photometrically Consistent Reconstruction

Qi Shao et al.
Under Review, 2026

Project Page

The first RGB-only multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM for collaborative photorealistic scene reconstruction. We introduce compact submap communication, geometry- and appearance-aware loop verification, and occupancy-aware Gaussian fusion, together with ReplicaMultiAgent Plus - a benchmark for collaborative Gaussian SLAM.

Research Experience

3D Reconstruction and SLAM

Dense mapping and robust localization for robotic systems, including monocular multi-agent 3D Gaussian Splatting SLAM and factor-graph-based visual-inertial-LiDAR localization.

Multi-Source Navigation and Fusion

Gravity and terrain matching navigation, satellite clock bias product combination, Bayesian estimation, and calibration methods for GPS-denied or disturbed environments.

Computer Vision for Intelligent Systems

Visual segmentation for small targets in complex scenes, including Mask R-CNN-based segmentation enhanced with PANet and Efficient Channel Attention.
